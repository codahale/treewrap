\title[TW128]{TW128: A Tree-Structured Duplex Authenticated Encryption Mode}
\author{Coda Hale}
\institute{Hale Institute for Bicycle Appreciation}

\keywords{authenticated encryption \and committing security \and sponge constructions \and duplex constructions \and Keccak}

\begin{abstract}
  We present \TW{}, a nonce-based authenticated encryption mode with
  associated data built as a two-level tree of keyed duplexes over the
  \Keccakp{} permutation. A serial root duplex binds the key, nonce, and
  associated data, encrypts the first message chunk, and aggregates a
  contribution from every later chunk into one authentication tag; those chunks
  are handled by independent leaf duplexes that, once the key and nonce are
  fixed, share no state and can be evaluated in parallel across SIMD lanes. We
  analyze \TW{} in the public permutation model, proving each bound first
  against an intermediate random oracle and then transferring it to the real
  permutation by the flat sponge lift. We prove a concrete multi-user
  indistinguishability bound and derive all-in-one authenticated encryption as
  its single-user corollary, together with bounds for the commitment security
  of the root tag, which we obtain by proving that the wrapper returning the tag
  is a secure hash of the $(K, N, A, M)$ context---collision-,
  second-preimage-, and preimage-resistant in the standard hash-function
  sense. The single root tag is thus a compact commitment; full-input
  committing security (\textsf{CMTDs-4}) follows as a corollary, and we also
  establish context discoverability. At the implemented parameters these
  notions meet a $128$-bit security target. Vectorized \texttt{amd64} and
  \texttt{arm64} implementations exploit the tree's leaf parallelism, exceeding
  $10$\,Gbit/s and coming within a factor of $1.3$ to $2$ of
  hardware-accelerated AES-128-GCM.
\end{abstract}
