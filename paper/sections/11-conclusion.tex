\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

\TW{} is a two-level tree of keyed duplexes over a single
\Keccakp{} permutation that combines mode-level parallelism,
constant memory, and commitment security in both its collision and
preimage facets. Its concrete public-permutation bounds---for all-in-one
AE, multi-user indistinguishability, $s$-way full-input committing
security (\textsf{CMTDs-4}), the tag-committing strengthening in which the
root tag alone is the compact commitment, and context discoverability in
both its tag-target (\textsf{T-CDY}) and \cite{MLGR23} forms---meet a
$128$-bit target at the implemented parameters,
and vectorized
\texttt{amd64} and \texttt{arm64} implementations turn the tree's leaf
parallelism into multi-gigabyte-per-second throughput within a small
factor of hardware-accelerated AES-128-GCM.

Several questions remain open (\Cref{sec:limitations}): a nonce-misuse-resistant
variant, security under the release of unverified plaintext, and sharper
bounds---whether by
recasting the leaf and root duplexes on the modern keyed-duplex analysis or
through a beyond-birthday argument. More broadly, \TW{} adds to the growing
evidence that a single, well-scrutinized \Keccak{} permutation suffices to build
authenticated encryption that is at once fast, parallel, and committing.
