\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

\TW{} is a two-level tree of keyed duplexes over a single \Keccak[1600, 12]
permutation that combines mode-level parallelism, constant memory, and
commitment security in both its collision and preimage facets. We analyzed it in
the public-permutation model, proving concrete bounds for all-in-one
authenticated encryption, multi-user indistinguishability, the strongest
$s$-way full-input committing notion (\textsf{CMTDs-4}), and context
discoverability by reasoning first against an intermediate random oracle and
then lifting through the flat-sponge transform; at the implemented parameters
all four meet a $128$-bit target. The accompanying \texttt{amd64} and
\texttt{arm64} implementations turn the tree's leaf parallelism into
multi-gigabyte-per-second throughput, within a small factor of
hardware-accelerated AES-128-GCM and with a ciphertext that is independent of the
degree of parallelism actually used.

Several questions remain open (\Cref{sec:limitations}): a nonce-misuse-resistant
variant, security under the release of unverified plaintext, and sharper
bounds---whether by
recasting the leaf and root duplexes on the modern keyed-duplex analysis or
through a beyond-birthday argument. More broadly, \TW{} adds to the growing
evidence that a single, well-scrutinized \Keccak{} permutation suffices to build
authenticated encryption that is at once fast, parallel, and committing.
