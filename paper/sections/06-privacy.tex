\section{Privacy}
\label{sec:privacy}

This section proves the random-oracle privacy bound feeding the
all-in-one AE composition of \Cref{thm:ae}. The public-permutation
privacy statement follows by the flat-sponge lift of
\Cref{app:flat-sponge-lift}.

\begin{theorem}[Random-Oracle Privacy]
\label{thm:priv-ro}
For every nonce-respecting privacy adversary $\mathcal{B}$ in the
\TW{} random-oracle model (\Cref{sec:ro-model}),
\[
  \Adv^{\mathsf{priv}}_\RO(\mathcal{B})
  \;\leq\; \KeyGuess_k(\qRO(\mathcal{B}))
  \;=\; \qRO(\mathcal{B}) / 2^k.
\]
Equivalently, the resource-indexed envelope satisfies $\Epspriv(\QRO)
\leq \KeyGuess_k(\QRO)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $\mathsf{P}_0$ be the real-side random-oracle privacy game: the
challenger samples $K \sample \bits^k$, $\mathcal{B}$ has direct access
to $\ROflat$, and accepted construction-interface queries are answered
by $\Enc_K^\RO$. Let $\mathsf{P}_1$ be the corresponding ideal-side
game, augmented with an independent auxiliary sample
$K \sample \bits^k$: construction-interface queries are answered by
$\Rand$, which returns a fresh uniformly random byte string of length
$\|M\| + \tauroot/8$, and direct queries are answered by the same lazy
$\ROflat$ interface. The auxiliary key is used only for the abort
predicate below and is ignored by $\Rand$, so the adversary's marginal
view is the ideal term in the definition of
$\Adv^{\mathsf{priv}}_\RO(\mathcal{B})$.
Let $q_e$ be an execution-uniform cap on the number of accepted
encryption queries made by $\mathcal{B}$, as in
\Cref{sec:resources}; this cap indexes the finite replacement
hybrid sequence and will not enter the final privacy bound.

Use the common abort event $\badkey$: a direct
adversary query $(Y,\ell)$ to $\ROflat$ satisfies
\[
  \KeyedTWOut(Y) = (K,\mathcal{C})
\]
for this game's key $K$ (the hidden key in $\mathsf{P}_0$, the
auxiliary key in $\mathsf{P}_1$) and some counted output-point class
$\mathcal{C}$ of \Cref{tab:keyed-twout}; the requested length $\ell$ is
ignored.

Let $\mathsf{P}_0^{\mathrm{stop}}$ and
$\mathsf{P}_1^{\mathrm{stop}}$ be the immediate-abort variants of
$\mathsf{P}_0$ and $\mathsf{P}_1$ on this event. By the Privacy
Stopped-Game Advantage Bound (\Cref{lem:priv-stopped-bound}) applied to
the event $\mathcal{B} \Rightarrow 1$,
\[
  \Adv^{\mathsf{priv}}_\RO(\mathcal{B})
  =
  \bigl|
    \Pr[\mathcal{B}^{\mathsf{P}_0} \Rightarrow 1]
    - \Pr[\mathcal{B}^{\mathsf{P}_1} \Rightarrow 1]
  \bigr|
  \leq \KeyGuess_k(\qRO(\mathcal{B})).
\]
Taking the supremum over nonce-respecting privacy adversaries with
$\qRO(\mathcal{B}) \leq \QRO$ gives the envelope statement
$\Epspriv(\QRO) \leq \KeyGuess_k(\QRO)$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Lineage.}
The proof is the flat-random-oracle analog of the standard
SpongeWrap privacy argument: stop on direct reads of hidden-key
construction transcripts, replace each nonce-respecting encryption
query by fresh random output under transcript freshness, and bound the
stop event as an adaptive key test. The \TW{}-specific interfaces are
Transcript Freshness (\Cref{lem:freshness}) and the privacy accounting
lemmas
(\Cref{lem:priv-one-query-replacement,lem:priv-stopped-bound}).
